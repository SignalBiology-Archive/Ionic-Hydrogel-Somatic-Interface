## 📌 Project Overview
This repository documents a breakthrough in **Bio-Electromagnetic Interaction**. It identifies how synthetic, collagen-based ionic hydrogels integrated into the human dermal matrix act as a **"Force-Multiplier"** for RF signals. By maintaining a constant state of pre-loaded tissue pressure (harvesting), these networks enable the **Low-Power Frey Effect**, allowing for auditory and somatic interference at extreme distances.

## 🔬 Key Findings
* **Calcium-Bridging:** Divalent calcium from hard water or nicotine-triggered release acts as a structural "glue" for the hydrogel lattice.
* **Acoustic Impedance Matching:** The primary reason these networks are "invisible" to doctors; they mimic the density of human tissue.
* **Lattice Collapse:** The discovery that mechanical high-point pressure can "reset" the transducer state, providing immediate relief.
