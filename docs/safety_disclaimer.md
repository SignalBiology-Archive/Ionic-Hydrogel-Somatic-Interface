
# Safety Disclaimer and Experimental Guidelines

**IMPORTANT:** This repository contains experimental biophysical research and protocols. By accessing these files, you acknowledge that you understand the risks associated with electromagnetic, chemical, and metabolic experimentation.

---

## ⚠️ 1. Electrical Safety: Galvanic Shunting
The protocols described in `hardware-specs/` involve direct electrical contact with the human body.
* **Mains Power Warning:** Never connect shunting arrays or conductive fabrics directly to wall outlets or mains-powered devices.
* **Grounding Source:** Use only dedicated ESD (Electrostatic Discharge) grounding rods or verified cold-water pipe grounds.
* **Current Limiting:** A resistor of appropriate value (e.g., 100k Ohm or higher) must be placed in series between the body and the ground to minimize the risk from ground loops or accidental surges.

## 🧪 2. Chemical Safety: Chelation Agents
The use of Citric Acid and other chelators (EDTA, Sodium Citrate) involves pH-level manipulation of the skin.
* **Concentration:** Do not exceed low concentrations for topical applications without prior patch testing.
* **Ocular Safety:** Citric acid is a severe eye irritant. Ensure all facial applications (Zygomatic nodes) are performed with protective eyewear or specialized application tools.
* **Ingestion:** Research protocols regarding mouthwashes or ingestion are theoretical. Consult a toxicologist before internal application of non-food-grade chelators.

## 🩺 3. Metabolic & Medical Disclaimer
The **"Insulin Reset"** and other metabolic observations involve complex physiological systems.
* **Not Medical Advice:** This research is for biophysical data collection, not for the treatment of any disease or condition.
* **Hypoglycemia Risk:** Never attempt to manipulate insulin levels (via diet or medication) without a continuous glucose monitor (CGM) and professional medical supervision.
* **Endocrine System:** Experimental metabolic shifting can have long-term effects on the endocrine system.

## 🛡️ Laboratory Code of Conduct
* **Reproducibility:** Document all ambient RF levels (using a spectrum analyzer) and humidity levels before testing, as these variables significantly affect hydrogel conductivity.
* **Integrity:** Report all findings, including "null results" where mitigation failed to show an effect.
* **Ethics:** All human participation in these experiments was voluntary, with informed consent regarding the nature of RF-induced somatic interference.
