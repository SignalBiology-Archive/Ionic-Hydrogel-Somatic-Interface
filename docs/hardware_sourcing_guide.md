# Hardware Sourcing Guide: High-Conductivity Materials on a Budget

**Objective:** To provide researchers and subjects with verified, low-cost sources for professional-grade mitigation hardware.

---

## 1. Conductive Textiles (The Grounding Shield)
**Requirement:** Ripstop Copper-Nickel (CuNi) Fabric.
* **The "Temu/AliExpress" Advantage:** Professional-grade CuNi fabric (often labeled as "EMF Shielding Fabric") is available for approximately **$10–$15 USD**.
* **Specifications:**
    - **Material:** Polyester + Copper + Nickel.
    - **Surface Resistance:** < 0.05 Ohm/sq.
    - **Shielding Effectiveness:** 70dB–90dB (Frequency range: 10MHz–30GHz).
* **Usage:** Use 3-fold layers for a total Faraday effect. Ensure direct dermal contact for shunting.

---

## 2. Dermal Grounding Nodes (Copper Tape)
**Requirement:** High-purity conductive adhesive copper foil tape.
* **Source:** Available at most hardware stores or online for **$5–$8 USD**.
* **Measurement:** 20mm to 50mm width is optimal for creating "Small Node" contact points on the skin.
* **Note:** Ensure the adhesive is also conductive to maintain the path to ground.

---

## 3. Active Dampening (HackRF One)
**Requirement:** Software Defined Radio (SDR) with Transmit (TX) capability.
* **Source:** Open-source HackRF clones are available on budget platforms for **$80–$120 USD**.
* **Measurements:** Must be calibrated to **-32dB** output power to avoid the "Stress Paradox" mentioned in the Active Interference protocol.
